## 2 · Phone script + voicemail

**Opener (gatekeeper → decision-maker):**
> "Hi, this is {{REP FIRST NAME}} with Tuned Yota. Could you point me to whoever handles
> your F&I or fixed-ops side? It's a quick partnership question, not a sales call."

**Live pitch (30 seconds):**
> "I won't take your time — I'll be quick. We do professional Toyota/Lexus truck calibration,
> but the reason dealers work with us is the documentation: emissions stay 100% factory,
> nothing gets defeated, every job is 5-gas verified with a serialized certificate. {{HOOK}}
> I'd like to drop off a folder and show you how it stays off your desk as a compliance
> worry. Fifteen minutes this week — {{DAY}} or {{DAY}}?"

**Voicemail (20 seconds):**
> "Hi {{DECISION-MAKER}}, {{REP FIRST NAME}} with Tuned Yota — we're the emissions-intact,
> fully-documented calibration shop for Toyota trucks. I've got a one-folder partner kit I'd
> like to leave with you; it's a referral relationship, zero cost or training on your end.
> I'll follow up by email. [Rep name], Tuned Yota, (612) 406-7117. Thanks."

**If they push back — quick objection map** (full versions in `walkthrough.md`):
- *"Corporate wouldn't like it."* → "Fair — that's exactly why we start at referral only, and
  why emissions stay factory and everything's documented. Nothing here obligates the store."
- *"Doesn't it void the warranty?"* → "That's the biggest myth — I've got a one-pager on it.
  Emissions are intact and the burden's on the warrantor to tie a failure to the work. It's
  education, not a promise — I'll leave it with you."
- *"Who backs the tune?"* → "OTT authors the calibration; we apply and document it. I can walk
  you through their support terms."
