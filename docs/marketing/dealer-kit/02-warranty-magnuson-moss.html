Warranty Education

Professional calibration and the factory warranty.

The most common dealer concern is "does this void the warranty?" The honest, careful answer starts with federal law.

The Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, in plain terms

Under the federal Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act, a manufacturer generally cannot void a vehicle's entire warranty simply because an aftermarket part or service was used. If a warranty claim is denied, the burden is on the warrantor to show that the specific aftermarket modification actually caused the specific failure being claimed.

This reframes the risk: not "a tune voids the warranty," but "a warrantor would have to tie a particular failure to the calibration" — and only the components demonstrably affected are ever in question, if any.

Why emissions-intact calibration lowers the risk further

Because our calibrations leave emissions hardware and monitors in place and change parameters within the factory hardware envelope, the surface area for a causation argument is narrow and documented. Every vehicle receives a serialized Certificate of Calibration recording exactly what was applied.

Who stands behind the calibration

The calibration itself is authored by Overland Tailor Tuning (OTT) and applied by Tuned Yota's authorized installers.
