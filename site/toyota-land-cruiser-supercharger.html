Land Cruiser supercharger FAQ

The Magnuson TVS2650 system for the 2008–2021 Land Cruiser 200 Series 5.7L is $8,395 at current Magnuson retail, with calibration, fuel pump and injectors included. The MP90 Classic for the 1995–1997 80 Series is $7,995. Installation and OTT calibration are quoted separately by Tuned Yota.

Magnuson rates the TVS2650 system at 543 horsepower and 535 lb-ft at the crank on 7 psi — versus 381 hp stock. The character of the truck changes completely: it stops feeling heavy.

Yes — the 5.7L TVS2650 carries CARB EO D-488-69 (50-state legal) and the 80 Series Classic kit carries EO D-488-71. Both include Magnuson's 3-year/36,000-mile system warranty, with a limited powertrain warranty available on eligible vehicles.

Yes — Magnuson's Classic Series MP90 for the 1995–1997 4.5L 1FZ-FE (and 1996–1997 Lexus LX450) is in the current catalog at $7,995. It's a complete bolt-on that runs on the factory ECU — no reprogramming required — and delivers about 25% more power and torque.

The 3UR-FE runs enormous engineering margins and the Magnuson system is an intercooled, sealed, OEM-style bolt-on with a factory warranty. What protects the engine is a proper calibration matched to your fuel and load — exactly what our in-house OTT calibration does.
