Why the Tacoma is the classic Magnuson build

  • The 3.5L 2GR-FKS makes its power high in the revs — great on paper, frustrating off-road. The TVS1900 fills in the bottom of the tach with instant, no-lag boost
  • 370+ hp / 330+ lb-ft at the crank per Magnuson — and roughly 246 → 362 wheel horsepower on our own dyno with install and OTT calibration
  • Direct bolt-on with no cutting: intercooled, maintenance-free (sealed rotors — no oil changes), factory fit and finish
  • Calibration is in the box, it's CARB 50-state legal (EO D-488-59), and Magnuson backs it with a 3-year/36,000-mile system warranty
  • Second-gen 4.0L trucks get the proven MP90 — 305 hp/335 lb-ft, injectors and programmer included, EO D-488-40
  • Want it to look as serious as it runs? Tacoma kits can swap to a colored lid (+$300) in blue, green, orange or red

First-gen and T100: the TRD-era blower, reborn

Magnuson built the original TRD supercharger, and its modern successor lives on: the TVS1320 retrofit for the 3.4L 5VZ-FE (1997–2004 Tacoma, 1997–1998 T100) packs 30% more displacement than the old MP62, makes 260+ hp and 260+ lb-ft, and runs on your stock ECU — no tune required. $4,495 at retail, CARB EO D-488-57.
